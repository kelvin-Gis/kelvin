GIS Analysis

GIS Analysis involves capturing, storing, analyzing, and visualizing spatial data to solve real-world problems such as urban planning, environmental monitoring, and infrastructure development.

Example Use Cases:

  • RSU site selection for telecom networks
  • Urban growth analysis
  • Disaster risk mapping
Remote Sensing

Remote sensing is the science of obtaining information about the Earth without physical contact, using satellites and aerial sensors.

  • Land use and land cover classification
  • Environmental change detection
  • Vegetation and water monitoring
Spatial Analysis

Spatial analysis examines patterns, relationships, and trends in geographic data.

  • Hotspot analysis
  • Buffer and overlay analysis
  • Location suitability modeling
Telecom GIS

Telecom GIS focuses on planning, designing, and optimizing communication networks using spatial data.

  • Fiber network planning
  • Cell tower coverage analysis
  • RSU site selection
